# S053: Relative Price of Finance

**Chapter 10: Competition, Finance, and Interest Rates** | **Figures**: Fig10.7 | **Period**: 1857–2011

## What This Measures

Computes the ratio of nominal interest rate to price level (i/p) from S052 outputs. The relative price of finance represents the real cost of borrowing in terms of commodity purchasing power. Higher i/p means finance is more expensive relative to goods.

Chapter 10 examines the relationship between finance, interest rates, and the real economy. Shaikh argues that the interest rate is not determined by supply and demand for loanable funds but by the competitive equalization between financial and industrial rates of return.
