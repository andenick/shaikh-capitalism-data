**Chapter 12: The Rise and Fall of the Phillips Curve** | **Period**: 1948–2010

## What This Measures

Load from the source data file ch12/Appendix12_CreditInflUnempl.csv

Chapter 12 traces the rise and fall of the Phillips curve as an empirical regularity. Shaikh demonstrates that the apparent inverse relationship between unemployment and inflation held only briefly in the 1960s, broke down during stagflation, and has been unreliable since — because the relationship has no sound theoretical foundation.
