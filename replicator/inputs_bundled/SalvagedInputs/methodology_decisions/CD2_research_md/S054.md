# S054: Real Interest Rate HP-filtered

**Chapter 10: Competition, Finance, and Interest Rates** | **Figures**: Fig10.8 | **Period**: 1857–2011

## What This Measures

Real interest rate = nominal bond yield - PPI inflation. HP filter applied with lambda=3 to extract the trend component. The HP filter smooths the series to separate cyclical fluctuations from the underlying trend. Lambda=3 is appropriate for annual data.

Chapter 10 examines the relationship between finance, interest rates, and the real economy. Shaikh argues that the interest rate is not determined by supply and demand for loanable funds but by the competitive equalization between financial and industrial rates of return.

> ### Classical Theory of Interest
The interest rate is the price of loan capital, determined by the competitive profit rate. Classical economics (Smith, Ricardo, Marx) argued that interest rates are regulated by the profit rate, not by the money supply (quantity theory).
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Shaikh (2016), Ch. 10
