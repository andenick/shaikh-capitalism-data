# S009: US Unemployment Rate

**Chapter 2: Turbulent Trends and Hidden Structures** | **Figures**: Fig2.7 | **Period**: 1890–2025

## What This Measures

Unemployment rate series constructed from BEA Long Term Economic Growth (1966, Series B1-B2) for 1860-1970 and Economic Report of the President for 1948-2010. The two series are spliced to form a continuous annual unemployment rate. Both sources report civilian unemployment as a percentage of the labor force. S009A = LTEG, S009B = ERP; final S009 is the spliced series.

## From the Book

> Civilian unemployment rate 1860-1970 from BEA (1966, Series B1-B2), and from 1948 to 2010 from the Economic Report of the President, table b-40.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Appendix 2.1, Figure 2.7

## Extension

- **API**: FRED `UNRATE`
- **Method**: direct splice at 2010
- **Continuity**: Same BLS civilian unemployment rate, identical units
- **Source**: https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/UNRATE

Extension via FRED UNRATE. FRED UNRATE is the same BLS civilian unemployment rate reported in the ERP. Direct continuation with identical units (percent of civilian labor force). No vintage issues. UNRATE is the same conceptual series. Direct splice (no reindexing needed).
