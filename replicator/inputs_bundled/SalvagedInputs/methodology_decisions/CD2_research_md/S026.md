# S026: Corporate and Non-Corporate Profit Rates

**Chapter 6: Capital and Profit** | **Figures**: Fig6.1, Fig6.4, Fig6.5 | **Period**: 1947–2024

## What This Measures

Sectoral profit rates calculated as net operating surplus (NOS) divided by lagged net capital stock. Corporate profit rate (rcorp) uses imputed-interest-adjusted NOS and current-cost net fixed capital stock from BEA Fixed Asset tables. Noncorporate profit rate (rnoncorp) applies the same methodology to the noncorporate sector, treating the WEQ2 wage equivalent as labor compensation for proprietors.

## From the Book

> Corporate and non-corporate profit rates from corrected NOS / lagged total capital. Data from BEA NIPA and Fixed Asset tables. Extension pre-computed via pull_bea_ch06.py from updated BEA tables.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Appendix 6.1/6.8.I, Figure 6.1
