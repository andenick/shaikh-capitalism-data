# S084: Classical and Conventional Phillips-Type Curves, 1948-1981

**Chapter 15: Modern Money and Inflation** | **Figures**: Fig15.8 | **Period**: 1948–1981

## What This Measures

Ch15 Fig15.8: Classical and conventional Phillips-type curves 1948-1981. Data constructed from Shaikh appendix source tables using the methodology described in Appendix 15.

Chapter 15 examines modern money and inflation. Shaikh argues that inflation is fundamentally driven by the growth of aggregate purchasing power relative to real output — not by the money supply, expectations, or cost-push factors. The credit system, not the central bank, is the primary engine of money creation.

## From the Book

> In keeping with Keynesian (and classical!) theory, capitalism is seen as subject
to recurrent cycles, booms and crashes. Fiscal policy is therefore needed to stabilize
the system, since monetary policy (deﬁned as interest rate stabilization) is unlikely to
be sufﬁcient in crises.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Shaikh (2016), Ch. 15

> Classical theories of the national price level

ment in chapter 14. The present chapter develops a classical approach to ﬁat-money
inﬂation.
>
