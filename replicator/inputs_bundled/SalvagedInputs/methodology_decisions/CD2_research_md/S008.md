# S008: Manufacturing Real Compensation

**Chapter 2: Turbulent Trends and Hidden Structures** | **Figures**: Fig2.6 | **Period**: 1780–2024

## What This Measures

Real compensation = nominal compensation / CPI. Seven columns with complex provenance: S008A (Sahr/MeasuringWorth CPI 1780-2010), S008B (US CPI 1982-84=100, Sahr 1780-1785 + MeasuringWorth 1786-2010), S008C (BLS Manufacturing Productivity output/hr index 1958=100), S008D (BLS manufacturing output/hr in 1958 dollars), S008E (BLS real unit labor cost 1889=100), S008F (BLS real compensation 1889=100), S008 (final: Mfg production worker real compensation = comp/hr / CPI, 1790-2010).

## From the Book

> Production worker real compensation for 1774-2010 based on manufacturing production worker nominal compensation (ec) in dollars/hr and Consumer Price Index (CPI) for 1774-2010 from Measuring Worth.com. Real compensation was derived as ec/CPI.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Appendix 2.1, Figures 2.5-2.6

## Extension

- **API**: MeasuringWorth `USCPI`
- **Method**: growth_rate splice at 2010
- **Continuity**: Nonfarm real compensation as growth proxy for manufacturing
- **Source**: https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/COMPRNFB

Extension via FRED COMPRNFB (growth-rate proxy). COMPRNFB (Nonfarm Business Sector: Real Compensation Per Hour) used as growth-rate proxy for manufacturing-specific real compensation. Post-2010 nonfarm compensation growth closely tracks manufacturing. Approximation: nonfarm used as manufacturing proxy because FRED has no manufacturing-specific real compensation series. Growth rates only borrowed, not levels. MeasuringWorth CPI (Officer & Williamson 2025) cached to 2025.
