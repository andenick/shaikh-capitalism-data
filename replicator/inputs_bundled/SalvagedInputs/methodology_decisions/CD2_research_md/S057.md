# S057: Equity Rate vs Corporate IROP

**Chapter 10: Competition, Finance, and Interest Rates** | **Figures**: Fig10.11 | **Period**: 1947–2011

## What This Measures

Shiller equity return deflated by BEA investment deflator (from S041). rreq = (dvr + delta_preq) / preq(-1), where dvr = real dividends, preq = real equity price. Compared to Ch6 S105 corporate IROP. Tests whether equity returns track the incremental rate of profit on corporate capital.

Chapter 10 examines the relationship between finance, interest rates, and the real economy. Shaikh argues that the interest rate is not determined by supply and demand for loanable funds but by the competitive equalization between financial and industrial rates of return.

## From the Book

> Figure 10.11: Equity Rate vs Corporate IROP
**Current-cost (real) equity rate of return**:
```
rreq_t = (dvr_t + (preq_t - preq_{t-1})) / preq_{t-1}
```
where peq, dv from Shiller (2014), converted to real terms using BEA gross investment deflator (Appendix 6.8.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Shaikh (2016), Ch. 10

> Fig10.11) vs average corporate rate of profit = current-cost NOS / corrected current-cost capital stock (S026-A from Chapter 6).
