# S062: Law of One Price at Aggregate Level, US and Japan

**Chapter 11: International Competition and the Theory of Exchange Rates** | **Figures**: Fig11.6 | **Period**: 1960–2009

## What This Measures

Load S200 (USJPNdata) Separate alternating Japan/US rows Extract S200E (LOP ratio) for each country

Chapter 11 develops a classical theory of exchange rates based on real competition. Shaikh shows that real exchange rates are governed by relative real unit labor costs (reflecting international competitiveness) rather than by purchasing power parity or interest rate differentials.

## From the Book

> Figure 11.6 depicts this ratio for both the United
States and Japan. Given the data limitations discussed earlier, and the large impact of
the anomalous 1980–1987 period, it is remarkable how stable this ratio is over the
long run.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Shaikh (2016), Ch. 11

> When applied to some aggregate bundles of goods in two different countries, this is
called the Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) hypothesis.
In real competition, the LOP encompasses transportation costs, taxes, and tariffs.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Shaikh (2016), Ch. 11
