# S079: Growth of Nominal GDP and Relative New Purchasing Power

**Chapter 15: Modern Money and Inflation** | **Figures**: Fig15.3 | **Period**: 1948–2011

## What This Measures

Ch15 Fig15.3: Growth of nominal GDP and relative new purchasing power. Data constructed from Shaikh appendix source tables using the methodology described in Appendix 15.

Chapter 15 examines modern money and inflation. Shaikh argues that inflation is fundamentally driven by the growth of aggregate purchasing power relative to real output — not by the money supply, expectations, or cost-push factors. The credit system, not the central bank, is the primary engine of money creation.
