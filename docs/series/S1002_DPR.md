## 1. Definition

**S1002** bundles two long historical macro series shown together on Figure 10.2:

- **S1002-A** (`iblong`): composite long-term high-grade corporate bond yield (percent), Macaulay railroad bonds 1857–1937 spliced into Moody's Aaa from 1938 onward
- **S1002-B** (`USWPI`): composite Producer/Wholesale Price Index (1947=100), Jastram (1977) before 1976 spliced into BLS PPIACO afterwards

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 10

Shaikh's Figure 10.2 demonstrates that the long-bond yield and the price level moved together over 150 years — the empirical foundation for treating the nominal interest rate as anchored by inflation expectations.

## 4. Construction

```
S1002-A_book[year] = USLR["iblong"][year]                       1857-2011
S1002-A_ext[year]  = FRED AAA annual mean                       2012-present
S1002-B_book[year] = USLR["USWPI"][year]  (already 1947=100)    1857-2011
S1002-B_ext[year]  = FRED PPIACO * scale  where scale=USWPI[2011]/PPIACO[2011]   2012-present
```

Reindex of PPI extension preserves the 1947=100 base via overlap anchor at 2011.

## 7. Caveats

1. **Jastram-to-BLS-PPI splice at 1976/1977.** Possible small level discontinuity under BLS WPI methodology revisions 1978/1986; absorbed by Shaikh into the book's `USWPI` column — we reproduce his values exactly.
2. **Extension uses fredgraph.csv endpoint** (per Phase 4): the JSON `/series/<id>` page rate-limits and required fallback; the csv endpoint is reliable and key-free.
3. **No proxy.** Modern AAA is the continuation of Moody's Aaa (same agency, same series). PPIACO is the continuation of the BLS PPI line Shaikh spliced.
