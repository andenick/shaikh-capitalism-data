## 1. Definition

**S1505** is the annual U.S. dataset underlying Shaikh's two Phillips-type scatter plots (Figures 15.6 and 15.8 in book):

- **Classical Phillips curve**: inflation rate `pi` vs growth-utilization gap `(1 - sigma')`
- **Conventional Phillips curve**: inflation rate `pi` vs civilian unemployment rate `uL`

Variables:
- `pi` = `ln(pGDP_t / pGDP_{t-1})` (GDP deflator inflation rate) from BEA NIPA T1.1.4
- `sigma` = `Igcorpbea / NOS` (Handfas 2012 construction), normalized to `sigma'`
- `uL` = BLS civilian unemployment rate (normalized to decimal)

Per Appendix 15.1 p. 896: `Igcorpbea` is current-cost corporate net fixed investment (BEA Appendix 15.2 working spreadsheet) and `NOS` is Net Operating Surplus from BEA NIPA T1.16.

## 2. Why it matters

Chapter 15 challenges the conventional NAIRU-based Phillips curve; the classical version (S1505 classical) shows a tighter, theoretically grounded relationship between inflation and the gap from a structurally determined growth ceiling.

## 7. Caveats

1. **BEA T1.16 row-label validation**: per Phase 4 Q4 resolution, T1.16 line ordering varies by vintage; the loader (when BEA API integrated) must validate by ROW LABEL "Net operating surplus", not numeric line position. The chopped values use Shaikh's 2012 vintage and are authoritative for replication.
2. **NOS vs gross-investment alternative**: Shaikh footnote 1 in Appendix 15.1 mentions an alternative sigma denominator (gross investment). We retain Handfas 2012 NOS for fidelity.
3. **uL unit normalization**: book values are decimal; BLS UNRATE on FRED is percent; loader must apply /100.
4. **No proxy**: BLS UNRATE, BEA NIPA — same sources Shaikh used.
