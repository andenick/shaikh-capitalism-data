## 1. Definition

**S1501** is the annual **U.S. Consumer Price Index, 1774-2011**, on a 1982-84 = 100 base. It is the canonical long-run U.S. consumer price level used in Shaikh (2016) Figure 15.1 ("Consumer Price Level, United States, 1774-2011").

The source — the MeasuringWorth USCPI (Officer & Williamson) annual reconstruction — splices the BLS CPI-U (1913-present) onto Warren-Pearson and historical retrospectives for the 1774-1912 segment, on a single index base (1982-84 = 100).

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 15

Chapter 15 ("Modern Money and Inflation") opens with a long view of the U.S. price level to motivate the regime distinction between commodity-money/gold-standard inflation and fiat-money inflation. Figure 15.1 highlights with shading the post-1933 period after the U.S. effectively left the national gold standard. The figure is the empirical backbone for Shaikh's argument that inflation behaviour changes qualitatively under fiat money.

## 4. Construction

S1501 is **direct** (Anu sense): a single observed annual index with a single forward-extension subsource on the same index base.

1. **Load** S1501-A (MeasuringWorth USCPI) for 1774-2011.
2. **Extend** (Phase 6) with S1501-B (FRED CPIAUCNS), reindexed to the MeasuringWorth value at the most recent overlap year (default 2011). Because both series share the BLS CPI-U as their post-1913 backbone on the same 1982-84 base, the scale factor is expected to be ~1.000.

**Formula** (none — direct, with overlap-anchor splice for extension):
```
S1501[year] =
  IF year in [1774, 2011] : MeasuringWorth_USCPI[year]
  IF year in [2012, 2025] : FRED_CPIAUCNS[year] * (S1501[overlap_year] / FRED_CPIAUCNS[overlap_year])
```

## 7. Caveats

1. **Pre-1913 reconstruction**: the 1774-1912 segment is Officer & Williamson's editorial splice of multiple historical series (Warren-Pearson wholesale price index reweighted to consumer proxy, etc.). The 1913+ segment is direct BLS CPI-U. We do not re-do the editorial splice — we use MeasuringWorth's published series as Shaikh did.
2. **License**: MeasuringWorth's permitted-academic-use license requires citing Officer & Williamson and prohibits commercial redistribution. The post-1913 BLS CPI-U (FRED `CPIAUCNS`) is the open-license fallback for the modern segment; MeasuringWorth's pre-1913 segment is what makes the long-run series unique.
3. **No proxy substitution**: Both subseries are CPI on the same base. The extension is the same BLS CPI-U that MeasuringWorth uses as its post-1913 backbone.
