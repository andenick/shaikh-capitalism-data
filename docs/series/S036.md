# S036: US Industry Incremental Rates of Profit

**Chapter 7: The Turbulent Equalization of Profit Rates** | **Figures**: Fig7.17 | **Period**: 1988–2005

## What This Measures

Incremental rates of profit (IROP) for 30 competitive US industries, 1988-2005. IROP_i = delta_PG_i / IG_i(-1), where PG = gross profits and IG = gross investment. Both PG and IG are invariant to the Capital Consumption Adjustment, making IROP independent of debatable depreciation assumptions and capital stock estimates.

Chapter 7 presents Shaikh's theory of real competition — the turbulent equalization of profit rates across industries. Unlike the neoclassical fiction of perfect competition, real competition is characterized by persistent profit rate differentials that oscillate around a common center, driven by capital mobility and the pursuit of maximum returns.

## From the Book

> The key empirical implication is that industry profit rates and incremental rates of profit (IROPs) will tend toward equalization — not in the sense of being equal at any point, but in the sense that their cross-sectional deviations will be stationary (turbulently gravitating around zero).
