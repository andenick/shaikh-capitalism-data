# S103: Personal Income Distribution below $200,000, Cumulative Probability from Above

**Chapter 17: Summary and Conclusions** | **Figures**: Fig17.2 | **Period**: 2011–2011

## What This Measures

Personal Income Distribution below $200,000, Cumulative Probability from Above (Fig17.2) covering 2011-2011. Constructed from IRS SOI 2011, income brackets below $200,000; IRS SOI 2011, survival function for bottom 97% (EPTC exponential).

Chapter 17 summarizes the book's main findings: capitalist economies are characterized by turbulent regulation (not equilibrium), driven by real competition (not perfect competition), with profitability as the central regulator of accumulation, growth, and crisis.
