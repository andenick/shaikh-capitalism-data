## 1. Definition

**S1604** is the annual **net** rate of profit (gross profit rate minus the 3-month T-bill rate) and the **net real incremental** rate of profit, US Corporations, 1947-2024, on a decimal-rate scale. **Figure 16.8** shows the two series plotted together to make the chapter's central empirical point: the Stagflation Crisis was precipitated when both rates fell to historical lows simultaneously.

S1604 also emits the wage-suppression-adjusted variant `rcorpalt` which uses the counterfactual EC path from **S1602** to compute what the profit rate would have been had real wages stayed on their 1960-1981 trend.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 16

The chapter's argument for the systemic, profitability-driven character of the 2007 crisis depends on showing that the underlying profit rate had been propped up since the 1980s by wage suppression. S1604 is the empirical anchor: the *net* (post-interest) profit rate and its difference from the counterfactual quantify how much of the post-1980s profit-rate recovery was attributable to held-down real wages rather than to genuine profitability gains.

## 4. Construction

S1604 is **composite** (difference series of two underlying chains). Construction:

```
r_corp_t        = S0608 corporate average profit rate (RSCD chapter-6 chain, decimal)
iroprcorp_t     = current real incremental profit rate (from chapter-6 chain)
i_3mo_t         = FRED TB3MS annual avg (decimal; converted from percent)

S1604-A_t  =  r_corp_t  - i_3mo_t                                 (net rate of profit)
S1604-B_t  =  iroprcorp_t - i_3mo_t                              (net incremental, raw)
S1604-C_t  =  HP100( S1604-B_series )_t                          (HP100-smoothed)
S1604-D_t  =  S1604-C_{t-1}                                       (HP100 lagged 1)

S1604-E_t  =  rcorpalt_t - i_3mo_t                               (counterfactual net rate)
  where rcorpalt_t = r_corp_t * (Pcorpalt_t / Pcorp_t)
  and   Pcorpalt_t derives from Eccorp_t * ec'_t = Eccorp_t * (ec_c_t / ec_t)   (from S1602)
```

**HP-filter parameter (Phase 4 ratified)**: `lambda = 100` for annual data (Shaikh's published choice). The Ravn-Uhlig `lambda = 6.25` is emitted as a sensitivity sidecar but is NOT the canonical Fig 16.8 series.

**HP endpoint fragility**: each extension shifts the HP100-smoothed values for the ENTIRE 1947+ series. This is a known structural property of two-sided HP filtering; the registry stamps `hp100_endpoint_fragility: true` so downstream consumers know smoothed values are not stable across runs.

## 7. Caveats

1. **HP100 lambda choice (canonical)**. Shaikh uses λ=100 for annual data — the Ravn-Uhlig 6.25 is a published academic alternative but is NOT what produces Fig 16.8. The canonical replication uses λ=100.
2. **HP endpoint instability**. Adding even one new annual observation shifts the entire smoothed series. This is unavoidable for two-sided filters; documented and flagged in registry.
3. **Cross-series dependency on S1602**. The `rcorpalt` subseries requires S1602's counterfactual `ec_c`. Phase 5 ordering S1602 -> S1604 is enforced by the run orchestrator (S1604 fails fast if S1602 has not yet produced `_sidecars/S1602_counterfactual_fit.json`).
4. **Cross-chapter dependency on S0606-S0608**. The corporate profit rate `r_corp` and incremental rate `iroprcorp` originate in the chapter-6 profit-rate chain. S1604 must consume RSCD-canonical outputs (`Technical/data/processed/S0608.parquet` etc.) — not the CD2 frozen values — to avoid double-counting in the cross-series uniqueness check.
5. **No proxies.** All components are primary BEA/BLS/FRB series.
