# S093: US and UK Golden Waves, 1786-2010

**Chapter 16: Growth, Profitability, and Recurrent Crises** | **Figures**: Fig16.1 | **Period**: 1786–2010

## What This Measures

Long-wave cycles in the ratio of wholesale prices to gold prices for the US and UK, 1786–2010. Computed as residuals of cubic time trends of PPI/gold ratios, estimated via EViews.

Shaikh's "golden waves" are central to his theory of long cycles in Chapter 16. The ratio of the general price level to the gold price reveals 50–70 year cycles that align with the major epochs of capitalist development: the pre-Civil War expansion, the post-Civil War deflation, the early 20th century boom, the interwar collapse, the postwar Golden Age, and the neoliberal era. Shaikh argues these long waves reflect the interaction of the falling rate of profit with periodic restructuring of the conditions of accumulation — each wave corresponds to a phase in which profitability first rises (through technical change, labor discipline, or new markets) and then falls as the inherent dynamics of competition reassert themselves.
