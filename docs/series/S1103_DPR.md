## 1. Definition

The ratio of the REER (PPI-basis) to the adjusted real unit labour cost ratio
for the United States (S1103-B) and Japan (S1103-A), annual 1960-2009.
Defined as `rxrrulcratio1 = rxr1 / rulcadjratio1rescaled`. Appears in Shaikh
(2016) as **Figure 11.6** (p. 531).

Conceptually: the deviation of the real exchange rate from its fundamental
(relative adjusted real unit labour costs). If the classical LOP holds at
the aggregate level, this ratio is stationary near 100.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 11

Figure 11.6 supports Shaikh's central empirical claim: REERs are tied to
relative unit labour costs (adjusted for tradable/nontradable composition).
The stable long-run ratio (despite the 1980-1987 anomaly) demonstrates that
real exchange rates gravitate around their RULC fundamentals.
