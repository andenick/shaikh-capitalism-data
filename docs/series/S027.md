# S027: Corrected vs Conventional Corporate Profitability

**Chapter 6: Capital and Profit** | **Figures**: Fig6.2, Fig6.6 | **Period**: 1947–2024

## What This Measures

Compares two measures of corporate profitability: (1) Corrected profit rate (rcorp) using imputed-interest-adjusted NOS and GPIM total capital stock (including IRS inventories); (2) Conventional NIPA profit rate (rcorpnipa) using standard NIPA corporate profits and BEA current-cost net stock. Also computes corresponding profit shares (NOS/VA for corrected, P_nipa/VA_nipa for conventional).

## From the Book

> Corrected vs conventional corporate profitability measures. r = r_NIPA x (x1/x2) x x3. Extension pre-computed from upstream S208 and S013.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Appendix 6.1/6.8.II.7, Figure 6.2
