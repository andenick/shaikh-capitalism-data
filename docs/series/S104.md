# S104: Personal Income Distribution above $200,000, Cumulative Probability from Above

**Chapter 17: Summary and Conclusions** | **Figures**: Fig17.3 | **Period**: 2011–2011

## What This Measures

Personal Income Distribution above $200,000, Cumulative Probability from Above (Fig17.3) covering 2011-2011. Constructed from IRS SOI 2011, income brackets at/above $200,000; IRS SOI 2011, survival function for top 3% (Pareto power-law).

Chapter 17 summarizes the book's main findings: capitalist economies are characterized by turbulent regulation (not equilibrium), driven by real competition (not perfect competition), with profitability as the central regulator of accumulation, growth, and crisis.

that the former dominates at lower income levels and the latter at the highest levels.

of the distribution of personal incomes in the United States is roughly exponential
while the top 1%–3% is roughly Pareto.
