# S223: Household Debt-Service Ratio Data (Source Table, Ch16)

**Chapter 16: Growth, Profitability, and Recurrent Crises** | **Period**: 1980–2012

## What This Measures

Parse Appendix16_HouseholdDebtService.csv (quarterly)

Chapter 16 synthesizes Shaikh's analysis of growth, profitability, and recurrent crises. The net rate of profit drives the rate of accumulation, which in turn drives growth. When profitability declines secularly, the system becomes increasingly fragile, producing periodic crises that are endogenous to capitalism — not caused by external shocks or policy errors.
