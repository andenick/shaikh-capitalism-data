## 1. Definition

Cross-sectional snapshot at Tax Year 2011: the cumulative probability from
above (survival function) of personal income (AGI), restricted to bins with
midpoint below $200,000 (the bottom 97% of returns). Appears in Shaikh (2016)
as **Figure 17.2** (p. 752), plotted on a log-linear scale to test for
exponentiality.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 17

The bottom 97% of the IRS 2011 personal income distribution is approximately
exponential — appears as a straight line on a log-linear plot. Combined with
the Pareto top tail (S1703), this validates the EPTC two-class framework
(Yakovenko et al.) and yields Shaikh's reported Gini of 0.492 (~0.50 = pure
exponential).

## 4. Construction

`composite`. From the IRS Table 1.4 long form (AGI bin -> number_of_returns),
the workbook computes:
1. Bin midpoint (arithmetic) for each AGI range.
2. Frequency = n_returns_i / total.
3. Cumulative frequency from below.
4. Cumulative frequency from above = 1 - (3).

S1702-A loads column "Cumulative Frequency from Above" (FPR017_C5 in CD2
naming) — Phase-4-ratified correction from the CD2 reference values which
had pointed to the Frequency column (FPR017_C3).

## 5. Year coverage

`year_range = [2011, 2011]` — single tax year, cross-sectional.

## 6. Units

`cumulative_probability_from_above_dimensionless`, plotted on a natural-log
y-axis vs bin midpoint in USD (arithmetic x-axis).

## 7. Caveats

1. **Column-fix.** Use column F (FPR017_C5), not column D (frequency) — Phase
   4 confirmed.
2. **Top open-ended bin '$10,000,000 or more'** has no midpoint in the
   spreadsheet and is dropped (handled in S1703 logic; S1702 filter naturally
   excludes it since midpoint < $200k threshold).
3. **No extension** — cross_sectional content_type; future tax years are
   separate series, not extensions.
