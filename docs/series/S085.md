# S085: Classical and Conventional Phillips-Type Curves, 1982-2010

**Chapter 15: Modern Money and Inflation** | **Figures**: Fig15.9 | **Period**: 1982–2010

## What This Measures

Ch15 Fig15.9: Classical and conventional Phillips-type curves 1982-2010. Data constructed from Shaikh appendix source tables using the methodology described in Appendix 15.

Chapter 15 examines modern money and inflation. Shaikh argues that inflation is fundamentally driven by the growth of aggregate purchasing power relative to real output — not by the money supply, expectations, or cost-push factors. The credit system, not the central bank, is the primary engine of money creation.
