## 1. Definition

**S1401** is the joint annual time series of (a) the **U.S. wage share** (Compensation of Employees divided by nominal GDP, decimal ratio) and (b) the **nominal GDP growth rate** (annual decimal). Both are plotted as the two axes of Shaikh (2016) Figure 14.10 over 1948-2011, alongside their HP(100)-filtered trends.

The wage share is the empirical analogue of the Classical share-of-value-added measure used throughout Chapter 14's wage-share Phillips curve.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 14

Figure 14.10 motivates Section IV's "broad concordance" claim: that the *level* of the wage share co-moves with the *growth rate* of nominal aggregate demand. This co-movement is the empirical anchor for the wage-share Phillips curve derived in Section V (Figs 14.13-14.14, our S1404-S1405) and ultimately for the real- and nominal-wage Phillips curves (Figs 14.16-14.17, our S1407-S1408).

## 4. Construction

S1401 is a **composite**: two derived annual time series sharing a common pair of NIPA Table 1.10 inputs.

**Formula** (verbatim, Appendix 14.2 p. 892):
```
wage_share_t       = EC_t / GDP_t           # decimal ratio
nominal_growth_t   = (GDP_t / GDP_{t-1}) - 1   # decimal annual growth rate
```
where `EC` = NIPA T1.10 line 2 (Compensation of employees, paid) and `GDP` = NIPA T1.10 line 1.

The processor (`P02_S1401_construct.py`) emits the Appendix-14.3 values verbatim for 1948-2011, then re-derives the same two formulas from FRED `A576RC1` and `GDP` for 2012+ and concatenates.

- Wage share: decimal ratio (e.g., 0.526 in 1948 → 52.6%)
- Nominal GDP growth: decimal annual rate (e.g., 0.102 in 1950 → +10.2%)
- HP(100) trends (separate plotting layer, not in the processed parquet) carry the same units.

## 7. Caveats

1. **Vintage drift**: BEA periodically revises NIPA. Replication should freeze a vintage via FRED ALFRED snapshot. We use the current FRED vintage in this build.
2. **Frequency mismatch**: FRED `GDP` is quarterly SAAR; we use FRED's annual aggregation (avg of 4 quarters) which matches Shaikh's annual-frequency convention.
3. **Composite, not direct**: the wage share is constructed inside the loader (Appendix 14.3 publishes the derived ratio); for the extension we must hold to Shaikh's exact ratio definition (no proxy substitution allowed).
