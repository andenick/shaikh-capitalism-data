# S017: GDP per Capita (Maddison)

**Chapter 2: Turbulent Trends and Hidden Structures** | **Figures**: Fig2.15, Fig2.16, Fig2.17 | **Period**: 1710–2000

## What This Measures

Maddison (2003) historical GDP per capita data. Decennial observations (10-year intervals) from 1710 to 2000. 206 columns covering world regions and individual countries. Key derived series: RICHEST 4 and POOREST 4 country averages. Kuwait, Qatar, and Venezuela are excluded from the top four when their inclusion would dramatically overstate the average (oil-rich distortion). Asia includes Japan, South Korea, oil-rich countries; Africa includes South Africa and Egypt - understates true rich-poor divergence.

Shaikh uses these series to demonstrate that capitalist economies exhibit turbulent growth with recurrent patterns — not smooth equilibrium. The data reveals persistent long waves, structural trends in production and investment, and systematic cyclical behavior that orthodox economics cannot explain.
