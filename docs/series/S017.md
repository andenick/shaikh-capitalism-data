# S017: GDP per Capita (Maddison)

**Chapter 2: Turbulent Trends and Hidden Structures** | **Figures**: Fig2.15, Fig2.16, Fig2.17 | **Period**: 1710–2000

## What This Measures

Historical GDP per capita from the Maddison (2003) dataset, with decennial observations from 1710 to 2000 for 206 countries and regions. The series constructs RICHEST 4 and POOREST 4 country averages to illustrate the great divergence in global living standards. Kuwait, Qatar, and Venezuela are excluded from the top four to avoid oil-rent distortions.

Shaikh uses this series (Figures 2.15–2.17) to document the widening gap between rich and poor nations over three centuries of capitalist development. The ratio of richest-to-poorest GDP per capita grew from roughly 2:1 in 1820 to over 20:1 by 2000, illustrating that capitalist growth, while generating unprecedented total output, systematically produces uneven development across nations — a pattern Shaikh traces to the dynamics of international competition analyzed in Chapter 11.
