# S028: Component Ratios x1, x2, x3

**Chapter 6: Capital and Profit** | **Figures**: Fig6.3 | **Period**: 1947–2024

## What This Measures

Three ratios decompose the relationship between corrected and conventional corporate profit rates. x1 = 1 + (NMINTcorp/Pcorp) captures the imputed interest adjustment. x2 = 1 + (INVcorp(-1)/KNCcorpbea(-1)) captures the inventory adjustment to capital stock. x3 = KNCcorpbea(-1)/KGCcorp(-1) captures the net/gross capital stock revaluation via GPIM. The relationship is: rcorp/rcorpnipa ≈ x3 * (x1/x2).

## From the Book

> Component ratios x1 (monetary interest), x2 (inventory), x3 (capital stock) accounting for difference between corrected and conventional measures. Note: x1 frozen post-2013 (NMINTcorp data incomplete).
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Appendix 6.1, Figure 6.3
