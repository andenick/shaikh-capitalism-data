## 1. Definition

**S1001** is the annual **Incremental Rate of Profit (IROP) for the Banking industry and for All Private Industries**, US 1988–2005 (two parallel time-series shown together on Figure 10.1 of Shaikh 2016). IROP for industry *i* is `IROP_i = ΔNOS_i / (IG_{i,t-1} + INV_{i,t-1})`.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 10

Chapter 10 ("Modern Money and Inflation") opens by arguing that financial-sector profit rates do *not* diverge systematically from industrial profit rates — banking competes for capital like any other industry. Figure 10.1 is the empirical anchor: bank IROP fluctuates around the all-private IROP. Shaikh references Appendix 7.2 (Ch7 industry-level S215 IROP panel) for the underlying derivation.

S1001 is `composite`: two parallel subseries assembled from the same Appendix 7.2 panel.

```

S1001-B[year] = Appendix7_iropdataUSind["All Private"][year]      for year in [1988, 2005]
```

No transformation; pass-through of the two columns Shaikh published.

## 7. Caveats

1. **CD2 mismatch in published validation values.** The CD2 markdown reported S050 spot values `1988=0.169, 1991=-0.037, 1994=0.122, 2003=0.017` that do not match the Appendix 7.2 `Banks` column (which shows `1988=-0.045, 1991=0.265, 1994=-0.147, 2003=0.123`). The dossier defers to Appendix 7.2 (the book's stated primary source). The CD2 values appear to come from a different vintage or definition; this is an informational divergence, not a Phase 5 blocker.
2. **NAICS reclassification.** Shaikh's 1988–2005 "banking" panel uses SIC-era classification with his own corrected capital-stock convention; the modern NAICS 5221 (Depository Credit Intermediation) is the recommended Phase 4 substitute for any future extension, with NAICS 52 (Finance & Insurance) as a broader sensitivity case.
3. **No extension in Phase 5 first pass.** Implementing the BEA NAICS 5221 extension requires constructing a Marx-equivalent capital stock correction analogous to App. 7.2 — analytically heavy, scheduled for Phase 6 sensitivity work per adequacy report.
