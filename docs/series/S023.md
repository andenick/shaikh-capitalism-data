# S023: US and UK Wholesale Price Indexes, 1790-2010

**Chapter 5: The General Price Level** | **Figures**: Fig5.4 | **Period**: 1790–2010

## What This Measures

Extract USWPI (S010F) and UKWPI (S010R) from S010, full range

Chapter 5 examines the general price level and the relationship between gold, money, and commodity prices. Shaikh argues that classical competition theory explains relative price movements through labor-time ratios, and that the general price level is governed by the money-commodity relationship, not by the quantity of money in circulation.

## From the Book

> Figure 5.4, which extends the time span to 2010, shows that these patterns change
dramatically after 1939/40. Prices rise more or less continuously in this new epoch,
and the previous stationary ﬂuctuations in national price levels are swamped by the
cumulative effects of persistent inﬂation.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Shaikh (2016), Ch. 5

ory of the general price level.
