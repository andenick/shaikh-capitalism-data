## 1. Definition

**S1005** plots three rates on Figure 10.5:

- S&P composite dividend yield (`ys` = D/P)
- 10-year US government bond yield (`ib10yr Gov`)
- composite long-term corporate bond yield (inherited from S1002-A)

## 2. Why it matters

Shaikh uses the dividend-yield-vs-bond-yield gap to motivate the "competition of capitals" argument: the equity dividend yield and bond yields move together over the long run because investors arbitrage across asset classes.

## 4. Construction

Pass-through of three published rates. The extension recomputes the Shiller dividend yield from the live `ie_data.xls` (annual average of monthly D/P) and pulls FRED GS10 + AAA for the bond rates.

## 5. Year coverage

- Book period: 1871–2011
- Extension: 2012–present

## 6. Units

`percent` for all subseries.

## 7. Caveats

1. **Shiller monthly→annual convention.** Phase 4 flagged this as needing confirmation at ingestion. We use the simple annual mean of monthly observations (matches DATAintropprice's apparent convention at spot-check years).
2. **`ie_data.xls` is the legacy OLE2 .xls format** — read with `xlrd` engine, not openpyxl.
3. **No proxy.** Shiller's published dividend yield is the same series Shaikh used; FRED GS10 and AAA are the same families.
