## 1. Definition

**S1601** is the annual de-trended price of commodities expressed in gold for the **United States** and the **United Kingdom**, 1786-2010, rebased to **1930 = 100** as residuals from a fitted cubic time trend on the log of `WPI / P_gold`. It is the Shaikh-Kondratieff "golden waves" decomposition that opens Chapter 16's argument that the 2007 global crisis was "on schedule" within long-wave price cycles.

In Shaikh (2016) the series appears as **Figure 16.1** ("US and UK Golden Waves, 1786-2010 — Deviations from Cubic Time Trends"). Data are physically located in **Appendix 5.3 Data Tables** (workbook `Appendix5_DATALRprices.xlsx`) which is shared with Chapter 5's gold-denominated price-of-commodities analysis.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 16

Chapter 16 opens by situating the 2007 financial crisis inside Kondratieff's long-wave framework: general crises and Great Depressions "typically begin in the middle of long downturns" of golden-denominated commodity prices (Shaikh 2016, p. 727). The golden-waves series, rebased to deviations from cubic trend, isolates the secular price-cycle signal stripped of the post-1933 inflationary drift introduced by the abandonment of the gold standard. The US and UK series have qualitatively similar wave structure with peaks around 1873 and 1929 and troughs around 1896 and 1933.

## 4. Construction

S1601 is **composite** with a two-variant emission:

**Baseline variant (`shaikh_1786_2010_published_trend`)** — uses Shaikh's published cubic-trend residuals (columns `USGoldWaveDetrended` and `UKGoldWaveDetrended`) directly from Appendix 5.3 for the entire 1786-2010 period. No re-estimation.

**Extension variant (`refit_cubic_full_sample_1786_2024`)** — re-fits the cubic on the extended `WPI_t / P_gold_t` sample:
```
ln(USPPIGold_t)  ~  beta_0 + beta_1 * t + beta_2 * t^2 + beta_3 * t^3   (t = 1786..2024)
residual_t       =  ln(USPPIGold_t) - fitted_t
dev_from_1930    =  residual_t - residual_{1930}
S1601-A_extended =  100 * exp(dev_from_1930)
```
Same procedure for UK. Both variants are emitted; the baseline is the "book-faithful" reference and the extended-sample re-fit is the modernised variant. Endpoint sensitivity is the documented structural fragility (see Caveats).

## 7. Caveats

1. **Cubic-trend endpoint instability.** Re-fitting the cubic on a longer sample shifts the residuals across the entire 1786-present range, not only at the endpoints. We emit BOTH the published-trend baseline (frozen 1786-2010 fit) and the re-fit variant, so users can choose between book fidelity and contemporary perspective. This is the documented Phase 4 resolution.
2. **Post-1971 floating gold volatility.** Gold de-pegged from USD in 1971; LBMA market price is dramatically more volatile than the pre-1971 official parity. The golden-wave signal is correspondingly noisier post-1971 — Shaikh acknowledges this in Appendix 5.3 notes.
3. **UK 1939-1945 gold price gap.** Jastram's historical UK gold price has no observations 1939-1945. Shaikh fills via NBER macrohistory UK WPI growth (NBER series `m04053`). This filled segment is documented in Appendix 5.3 but is an interpolative chain, not a fresh measurement.
4. **WPI methodology revisions.** BLS WPI/PPI was restructured in 1978 and again in 2014; ONS UK PPI K646 replaced the historical UK WPI in 1989. We use growth-rate splices at these junctures and document the level shifts in the EPR.
5. **No proxies.** All components are primary published series — US WPI (BLS / NBER), UK WPI (BoE Millennium / O'Donoghue), gold (London PM fix / LBMA). Cubic-trend de-trending is a methodological transform, not a proxy substitution.
