## 1. Definition

Cross-sectional snapshot at Tax Year 2011: the cumulative probability from
above (survival function) of personal income (AGI), restricted to bins with
midpoint at or above $200,000 (the top ~3% of returns). Appears in Shaikh
(2016) as **Figure 17.3** (p. 753), plotted on a log-log scale to test for
Pareto behaviour.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 17

The top 3% of the IRS 2011 personal income distribution is approximately
Pareto — appears as a straight line on a log-log plot. Pareto exponent
estimated by OLS on log(survival) vs log(midpoint).

## 3. Source

Same as S1702: IRS SOI Publication 1304 Table 1.4, Tax Year 2011 (via
`Appendix17_USIRS2011.xlsx`).

## 4. Construction

`composite`. Same pipeline as S1702, filter to bins with midpoint >= $200k.

## 6. Units

`cumulative_probability_from_above_dimensionless` on natural-log y-axis vs

## 7. Caveats

1. **Column-fix** — use FPR017_C5 not FPR017_C3 (Phase 4).
2. **Top bin '$10,000,000 or more' DROPPED.** The workbook has no midpoint
   (NaN) and `Cumulative Frequency from Above = 0` for this bin; per
   anti-synthetic rule we do not impute a midpoint. Phase 4 confirmed.
3. **Open-ended top-tail midpoints preserved verbatim** per Shaikh Appendix
   17.2: $500k-$1M -> 750,000; $1M-$1.5M -> 1,250,000; $1.5M-$2M -> 1,750,000;
   $2M-$5M -> 3,500,009 (note odd '9'); $5M-$10M -> 7,500,000.
4. **Pareto exponent recovery.** Shaikh does not publish alpha; the loader
   computes it via OLS in log-log space (slope = -alpha).
