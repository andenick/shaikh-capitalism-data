## 1. Definition

Annual exports-over-imports ratio (X/M) in common currency (USD) for each of
fifteen major OECD countries, 1960-2009. A ratio greater than one signifies a
trade surplus, less than one a deficit. Appears in Shaikh (2016) as **Figure
11.2**, panel showing each country's trade balance trajectory.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 11

Figure 11.2 motivates Shaikh's discussion of persistent trade imbalances and
the failure of standard PPP-based exchange-rate theories. The X/M ratio is the
"empirical anchor" before Shaikh introduces relative real unit labor costs.
