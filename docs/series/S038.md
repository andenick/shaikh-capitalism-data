# S038: OECD Industry IROP Deviations (PPP-adjusted)

**Chapter 7: The Turbulent Equalization of Profit Rates** | **Figures**: Fig7.19, Fig7.21 | **Period**: 1988–2003

## What This Measures

OECD industry incremental rates of profit (IROPs) and their deviations from the cross-industry average, 1988–2003. Uses the OECD STAN database for gross fixed capital formation (GFCF) and gross operating surplus (GOPS), converted to PPP-adjusted US dollars using Penn World Table 6.2. IROP = ΔGOPS / GFCF(-1), computed at the industry level after aggregating across countries.

This series provides the international evidence for Shaikh's theory of real competition. The key prediction is that profit rates across industries will exhibit *turbulent equalization* — not convergence to a single value, but stationary deviations around a common level, driven by capital mobility. The data covers ~30 OECD countries and 30 competitive industries (excluding nonprofits and high-wage-equivalent sectors), demonstrating that the profit-rate equalization mechanism operates across national boundaries, not just within a single economy.
