## 1. Definition

**S1502** is the set of annual **percent-change growth rates of real Gross Output for 8 U.S. major industries**, 1988-2010. It is panel A (goods & trade) of Shaikh (2016) Figures 15.2-15.3, computed as `g_i(t) = (YR_i(t) - YR_i(t-1)) / YR_i(t-1)` where `YR_i` is the BEA Chain-Type Quantity Index for Gross Output by Industry (2005=100 vintage at Shaikh's retrieval).

**Formula note**: although the research dossier originally cited a log-difference convention, empirical verification against Appendix15_USGDPRByIndustry.xlsx "Calculated Growth Rate" columns shows Shaikh uses simple percent change in this chapter. Log differences and percent changes agree to first order but diverge for large absolute changes (e.g. 1999 Utilities +17.85% vs ln-diff +16.42%).

Panel A industries (8): Agriculture/Forestry/Fishing/Hunting, Mining, Utilities, Construction, Durable Mfg., Nondurable Mfg., Wholesale Trade, Retail Trade. A 9th "All Industries" reference line is also emitted as the panel benchmark.

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 15

Chapter 15 documents cross-sectoral heterogeneity in real-output growth as background to its inflation analysis. The figures illustrate how broad sectoral dispersion (mining and construction swing widely while services grow steadily) shapes aggregate inflation dynamics under modern fiat money.

## 4. Construction

S1502 is **formula** (Anu sense): `g_i(t) = (YR_i(t) - YR_i(t-1)) / YR_i(t-1)` per industry per year. The chopped table contains both the chain-type quantity index levels (cols 1-19) and Shaikh's precomputed growth rates (cols 20-38) — the loader reads BOTH and the processor recomputes growth rates from the levels using simple percent change to match Shaikh's convention.

**Year boundary**: 1987 is the first level year; 1988 is the first growth-rate year (per adequacy review remediation).

## 7. Caveats

1. **Vintage stability**: BEA periodically revises base year (2005=100 at Shaikh's retrieval; modern vintage 2017=100). Growth rates are scale-invariant under base rebasing, so the rates are comparable across vintages.
2. **1987 missing**: 1987 is the level base — growth rate begins 1988.
3. **NAICS sector boundaries**: BEA reaggregated some sub-sectors (FIRE, Federal Gov) in revisions after 2010. The book panel uses Shaikh's 2012 vintage; post-2010 extension must splice carefully.
4. **No proxy**: same BEA Industry source as Shaikh.
