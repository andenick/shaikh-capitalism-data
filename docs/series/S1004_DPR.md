## 1. Definition

**S1004** = the real long bond yield (`iblongreal = iblong − π_PPI`, where `π_PPI` is the year-over-year PPI growth rate in percent) and its HP-filtered trend(s). Per Phase 4 ratification, the loader emits **two** HP smooths:

- `iblongrealHP3` (book label, but **numerically equivalent to λ=100**, see §7.4) — primary, book replication
- `iblongrealHP6.25` — λ=6.25 (Ravn-Uhlig 2002 standard for annual data, sensitivity)

## 2. Why it matters

Shaikh's Figure 10.4 shows the real interest rate fluctuating around a slow-moving trend — the empirical hook for the next chapter's argument about the real-interest-rate floor.

## 4. Construction

```
iblongreal[t] = iblong[t] − ( USWPI[t] / USWPI[t-1] − 1 ) * 100

HP filter: two-sided, default padding, λ ∈ {3, 6.25}, applied end-to-end on the
           full panel (no splice on the smoothed series — endpoint problem disclosed)
```

## 5. Year coverage

- 1857–2011 book; 2012–present extension; HP recomputed end-to-end whenever new years are appended.

## 6. Units

`percent` for all four subseries.

## 7. Caveats

1. **HP filter endpoint problem.** Adding new years shifts the smoothed trend near the splice point. This is mathematically unavoidable; documented in the EPR.
2. **No-Lazy-Splices rule.** The HP-filtered series is recomputed end-to-end; we never splice the smoothed trend.
3. **Two HP variants emitted.** λ=100 (book replication, see caveat 4) and λ=6.25 (Ravn-Uhlig standard) per Phase 4 ratification.
4. **Book label "HP3" actually corresponds to λ=100** (Phase 5 sweep verification). A lambda sweep against the published `iblongrealHP3` column in Appendix10_USLR.xlsx shows MAE = 0.0000 at λ=100 and MAE = 0.022 at λ=3. The column name appears to denote something other than the smoothness parameter Shaikh actually applied (possibly a cycle-frequency naming convention from a Mathematica HP variant). For exact numerical replication we use λ=100. The λ=6.25 sensitivity is unchanged.
