## 1. Definition

**S1603** is the annual short-term interest rate, 1960-2024, for the United States and a Shaikh-Ragab bespoke weighted average of major OECD countries. **Figure 16.7** ("US Interest Rate and Weighted Average of Major OECD Countries, 1960-2011") documents the secular post-1981 decline in policy-relevant short rates across the capitalist core.

**Dual-variant emission** (Phase 4 ratified):
- **S1603_replicated** — book-fidelity rebuild: Fed H.10 broad-index trade weights × IMF IFS country money market / T-bill rates (canonical).
- **S1603_oecd_mei** — low-effort approximation: OECD MEI IRSTCI01 pre-aggregated series (flagged `proxy: true` with explicit Concept Match Justification on the GDP-PPP vs trade-weight basket difference).

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 16

The chapter's argument that the 2007 crisis was the endpoint of a long policy-rate decline rests on showing this decline was a US-only or a global phenomenon. The dual US + OECD weighted-average chart in Fig 16.7 establishes the global character. The S1603 weighting reflects how much each OECD partner matters for US monetary-policy transmission — the same Fed H.10 trade weights used in TWI/REER calculations.

## 4. Construction

**S1603 is `composite`** with two parallel emission variants:

### Variant 1: `S1603_replicated` (book-fidelity)

```
ioecd_t = sum over countries c { w_{c,t} * i_{c,t} }
  where w_{c,t} = Fed H.10 broad-index trade weight for country c at year t (latest vintage)
        i_{c,t} = IMF IFS Money Market Rate (or T-Bill Rate) for country c at year t, annual avg
```
Latest-vintage Fed H.10 weights are applied to all years 1960-2024 (Phase 4 ratified — avoids weight churn).

### Variant 2: `S1603_oecd_mei` (low-effort approximation, `proxy: true`)

```
ioecd_t = OECD MEI IRSTCI01[year=t]   (pre-aggregated GDP-PPP-weighted basket)
```
Concept Match Justification: both measure "trade-weighted OECD short-term interest rates" but the weighting bases differ (Fed H.10 trade weights vs OECD GDP-PPP). Basket overlap is partial. Flagged `proxy: true`.

### US component
Direct continuation: book Appendix 16.2 `US` column (1960-2012) -> FRED `TB3MS` annual-avg (2013-2024) via direct splice (same agency / same release).

## 7. Caveats

1. **Bespoke OECD construction.** The book's OECD weighted average is Shaikh-Ragab original work, not an off-the-shelf series. Replication requires multi-country fetch and weighted aggregation. The OECD MEI variant is offered as a low-effort fallback with explicit proxy flag.
2. **gpoaccess.gov ERP URL is DNS-dead.** The original book citation `http://www.gpoaccess.gov/eop/tables10.html` returns DNS failure as of 2026-05-18. The Economic Report of the President has migrated to `https://www.govinfo.gov/app/collection/erp`. RSCD cites govinfo as the live provenance URL; FRED `TB3MS` is the practical extension data source.
3. **Trade-weight vintage decision.** Fed H.10 weights are themselves revised. RSCD uses latest-vintage weights for all years (Phase 4 ratified to avoid weight churn across years). Vintage-as-of methodology is documented as a sensitivity variant.
4. **No US proxy.** US rate is directly continued via FRED `TB3MS` which is the same St. Louis Fed redistribution of the US Treasury auction-result 3-month bill rate the book cites via ERP Table 73.
