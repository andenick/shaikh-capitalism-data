# S077: Growth Rates of Real Output, US Major Industries (Part 1)

**Chapter 15: Modern Money and Inflation** | **Figures**: Fig15.2A | **Period**: 1988–2011

## What This Measures

Growth rates of real output for major US industries (Part 1), 1988–2011. Industry-level real output data from BEA GDP-by-Industry tables, with growth rates computed as annual percentage changes.

Shaikh presents industry growth rates in Chapter 15 to demonstrate that individual industry dynamics are turbulent and heterogeneous even when the aggregate economy appears stable. The dispersion of growth rates across industries reflects the uneven development inherent in capitalist competition — some sectors expand rapidly while others stagnate or decline, driven by differential profitability and technical change. This micro-level turbulence is a key feature of "real competition" as opposed to the frictionless equilibrium of textbook models.
