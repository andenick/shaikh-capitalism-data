## 1. Definition

Three-line overlay chart for the United States, 1960-2009 (Figure 11.7,
p. 534):

- **S1104-A** US trade balance, net-trade ratio `(X-M)/(X+M)`.
  Phase 4 unit correction: CD2 negative sample values confirm this is the
  net-trade ratio, NOT the X/M ratio that S1101 uses.
- **S1104-B** US REER PPI-basis (same data as S1102-B; emitted here for
  self-contained chopped CSV).
- **S1104-C** US/EU12 relative real GDP, 2002=100 (NOT emitted in v1 —
  requires 12-country WDI fetch deferred to Phase 9).

## 2. Why it matters in Chapter 11

Figure 11.7 demonstrates that "the real exchange rate and relative GDP do
indeed pull in opposite directions" (p. 534). The deterioration of the US
trade balance is consistent with the improvement in its average competitive
level — a non-PPP fundamental driving long-run trade balances.
