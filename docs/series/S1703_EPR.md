## 1. Why this series cannot be extended

S1703 is a cross-sectional snapshot at a single tax year (2011). Per
anu-framework: "Extensions ONLY apply to time_series type." No extension is
performed.

A multi-year EPTC reconstruction (1996-2008 per Shaikh, Papanikolaou, Weiner
2014, cited at p. 753) would be a separate registry exercise — out of scope
for S1703.

## 2. Top-bin drop disclosure (anti-synthetic)

The open-ended `$10,000,000 or more` IRS bin has NO midpoint in the
Appendix 17.2 spreadsheet and `Cumulative Frequency from Above = 0` for that
bin. Per the No-Synthetic rule, we do NOT impute a midpoint (e.g.,
$15M or $50M) and we do NOT include the bin in the plotted survival points.
This matches Shaikh's own Fig 17.3 which terminates at the $5M-$10M bin.

## 3. Pareto exponent estimation

OLS in log-log space:
`ln(P(Y > y)) = a - alpha * ln(y)`
estimated over the six top-tail bins ($350k through $7.5M midpoints).
Slight non-monotonicity at $3.5M / $7.5M reflects IRS sample noise; we do
NOT smooth (the value is preserved verbatim from the workbook).

## 4. No-Proxy / No-Synthetic

The IRS data is itself the canonical authoritative source. Open-bin
midpoints come directly from Shaikh's Appendix 17.2 spreadsheet — preserved
verbatim, never re-derived. The $10M+ bin is excluded (no fabrication).
