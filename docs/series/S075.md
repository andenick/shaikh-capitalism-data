# S075: HP-Filtered Nominal Wage Growth vs Unemployment Intensity

**Chapter 14: The Theory of Wages and Unemployment** | **Figures**: Fig14.17 | **Period**: 1948–2011

## What This Measures

Ch14 Fig14.6: HP-filtered nominal wage growth vs unemployment intensity. Data constructed from Shaikh appendix source tables using the methodology described in Appendix 14.

Chapter 14 develops a classical theory of wages and unemployment. Shaikh shows that the real wage is determined by the balance of power between labor and capital, mediated by the reserve army of labor. The wage share oscillates around a persistent level, with unemployment intensity (not the unemployment rate alone) as the key regulator.
