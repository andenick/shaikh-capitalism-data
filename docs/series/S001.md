# S001: US Industrial Production Index

**Chapter 2: Turbulent Trends and Hidden Structures** | **Figures**: Fig2.1 | **Period**: 1860–2025

## What This Measures

A continuous index of US industrial production from 1860 to the present, constructed by splicing two sources: BEA Long Term Economic Growth (1966, Table A15) covering 1860–1918, and the Federal Reserve Board G-17 Industrial Production Index covering 1919 onward. Both series were rebased to 1958=100 and spliced at 1919.

Shaikh uses this series to illustrate the "apparently inexorable tendency toward growth" of capitalist economies, noting that "growth rates are not constant in the long run" — both industrial output and investment show higher average rates of growth in earlier epochs. The series also demonstrates that "growth is always turbulent," with the path of investment far more volatile than output. Shaikh argues that any adequate theory of growth must address these patterns of trending but turbulent accumulation.

## From the Book

> All Industries, 1860-1959 (1913 = 100) are from the BEA (1966, table A15), and 1919-2010 (2007 = 100) from the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System. The two series were rebased to 1958 = 100 and spliced at 1919.
>
> — Shaikh (2016), Appendix 2.1, Figure 2.1

## Data Sources

|-----------|--------|--------|-------|

| S001-B | Derived from S001-A | 1860–1918 | Index 1958=100 |
| S001-C | Federal Reserve Board, G-17 Industrial Production Index, All | 1919–2010 | Index 2017=100 |
| S001-D | Derived from S001-C | 1919–2010 | Index 1958=100 |

## Extension

- **API**: FRED `INDPRO`
- **Method**: growth_rate splice at 2010
- **Continuity**: Same underlying FRB G.17 Industrial Production Index
- **Source**: https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/INDPRO

Extension via FRED INDPRO. FRED INDPRO is the same FRB G.17 Industrial Production Index that Shaikh used. Direct continuation. FRED INDPRO base year changed from 2007=100 to 2017=100; growth-rate splice eliminates base-year dependency.
