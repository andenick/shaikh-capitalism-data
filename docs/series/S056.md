# S056: Bond and Equity Returns

**Chapter 10: Competition, Finance, and Interest Rates** | **Figures**: Fig10.10 | **Period**: 1926–2010

## What This Measures

Annual total returns for three major asset classes from Ibbotson (2004, Table 2-2): large company stocks (rslarge), long-term corporate bonds (rbcorplt), and long-term government bonds (rbgovlt), covering 1926–2010. Updated to 2010 by David Stubbs.

Shaikh uses this series in the context of his classical theory of finance (Chapter 10) to demonstrate that equity returns are fundamentally tied to the profit rate, not to efficient-market expectations. The long-run comparison of equity vs bond returns shows that the equity premium reflects the underlying profitability of real capital — equities command higher returns because they represent claims on profit-generating assets, while bonds represent loan capital whose return is regulated by competition with the profit rate.
