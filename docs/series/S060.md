# S060: Trade Balances in Major Countries

**Chapter 11: International Competition and the Theory of Exchange Rates** | **Figures**: Fig11.2 | **Period**: 1960–2009

## What This Measures

Load S201 (XMData) Pivot multi-country rows into per-country time series Extract trade balance ratio (S201) for US, UK, Germany, Japan, China

Chapter 11 develops a classical theory of exchange rates based on real competition. Shaikh shows that real exchange rates are governed by relative real unit labor costs (reflecting international competitiveness) rather than by purchasing power parity or interest rate differentials.

## From the Book

> Figure 11.2 depicts the trade balances of ﬁfteen
major countries from 1960 to 2009. These are measured as the export–import ratio in
common currency, so that a ratio greater than one signiﬁes a trade surplus and a ratio
below one signiﬁes a trade deﬁcit.
