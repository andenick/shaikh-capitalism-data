# S087: HP(100) Trend of Net Incremental Rate of Profit

**Chapter 15: Modern Money and Inflation** | **Figures**: Fig15.11 | **Period**: 1948–2011

## What This Measures

The Hodrick-Prescott filtered trend (lambda=100) of the net incremental rate of profit for the US corporate sector, 1948–2011. The HP filter extracts the long-run trend from the inherently volatile IROP series.

Shaikh uses the HP-filtered IROP trend in Chapter 15 to connect the dynamics of profitability to the theory of inflation. The smoothed profit rate trend reveals the secular decline more clearly than the raw IROP, which is dominated by year-to-year volatility. In Shaikh's framework, the trend profit rate is a key determinant of the long-run inflation rate: falling profitability creates pressure for firms to raise prices, while the interaction of credit expansion and profit squeeze generates the inflationary episodes observed in the 1970s and after.
